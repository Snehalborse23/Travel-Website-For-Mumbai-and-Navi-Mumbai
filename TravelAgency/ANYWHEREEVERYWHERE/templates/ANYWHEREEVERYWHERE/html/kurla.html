KURLA

Kurla is a suburb of East Mumbai, India. It is the headquarters of the Kurla taluka of Mumbai Suburban district. The neighbourhood is named after the eponymous East Indian village that it grew out of. It falls under Zone 5, Ward 'L' of the Bombay Municipal Corporation. Its railway station, spelt as Coorla until 1890, is one of the busiest on the Mumbai suburban railway on the central and harbour railway lines of Mumbai as is the Lokmanya Tilak Terminus (LTT) for out-station passenger/express trains.

-> Phoenix Mall

Opened in 2011, Phoenix Marketcity is the largest shopping mall in Mumbai. It is situated in Kurla and is spread across 4.1 million sq ft with 2.1 million sq ft of retail area. The mall houses over 600 international and national stores, over 100 restaurants, and PVR Cinemas which has 14 screens. It is one of the most popular mall complexes in the city. It has revolutionised the shopping experience for those who come to visit. It offers not only a place to shop but also a place where visitors can dine and get entertained, all in the same place. Visitors can watch movies, enjoy games at the arcade, and have splendid food. With the amalgamation of fashion, food, fun, ambience, and culture, Phoenix Marketcity harbours a new era for destination shopping for the entire family. Various events are organised in the Dublin Square area of the mall for all visitors to experience.
